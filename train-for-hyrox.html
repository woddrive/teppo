How to Train for the HYROX

Helpful tips on how to effectively prepare for the HYROX.


Build endurance

To build endurance for HYROX, you should incorporate cardio exercises such as running, cycling, or rowing into your training routine. You can start with shorter distances or durations and gradually increase them over time. For example, you can start with a 5k run and gradually increase it to a 10k or even a half-marathon. Interval training can also be effective, where you alternate between high-intensity efforts and periods of rest.

Focus on strength training

HYROX involves a lot of weightlifting, so it's important to include strength training in your routine. Focus on exercises that target the major muscle groups, such as squats, deadlifts, bench press, and pull-ups. You can also incorporate functional movements that mimic the events in HYROX, such as sled pushes, box jumps, and burpees. It's important to focus on proper form and technique to prevent injury.

Practice the events

To prepare for HYROX, it's important to practice each event. Set up a mock HYROX course and practice each event, focusing on form and technique. For example, you can practice the rowing event by using a rowing machine and focusing on your technique. You can also practice the sled push by pushing a weighted sled or a heavy object across a distance. Practicing the events will help you become more familiar with them and improve your performance on competition day.

Train for transitions

HYROX involves moving quickly between events, so it's important to train for transitions. Practice moving quickly from one event to the next, and focus on maintaining your form and technique. For example, you can practice moving quickly from the rowing machine to the sled push, or from the burpee broad jump to the sandbag carry. You can also practice changing your shoes and equipment quickly to save time (even though you probably won't need to come race day).

Rest and recover

HYROX is a demanding competition, so it's important to rest and recover properly. Make sure to get enough sleep, eat a healthy diet, and take rest days as needed to prevent injury and burnout. You can also incorporate recovery techniques such as foam rolling, stretching, and massage to help your muscles recover faster. It's important to listen to your body and not overtrain, as this can lead to injury and hinder your performance.
